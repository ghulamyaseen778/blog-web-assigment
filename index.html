Natural language processing

This article is about natural language processing done by computers. For the natural language processing done by the human brain, see Language processing in the brain.

Natural language processing (NLP) is an interdisciplinary subfield of linguistics, computer science, and artificial intelligence concerned with the interactions between computers and human language, in particular how to program computers to process and analyze large amounts of natural language data. The goal is a computer capable of "understanding" the contents of documents, including the contextual nuances of the language within them. The technology can then accurately extract information and insights contained in the documents as well as categorize and organize the documents themselves. Challenges in natural language processing frequently involve speech recognition, natural-language understanding, and natural-language generation.

1

Sentence segmentation

Sentence segmentation, also known as sentence boundary detection or sentence tokenization, is a crucial preprocessing step in Natural Language Processing (NLP). The goal of sentence segmentation is to divide a given text into individual sentences, as sentences are the basic units of meaning in written language. Accurately segmenting sentences is essential for many downstream NLP tasks, such as machine translation, sentiment analysis, and named entity recognition.

2

Word tokenization

Word tokenization, also known as word segmentation or word tokenization, is a fundamental natural language processing (NLP) task that involves breaking a given text or sentence into individual words or word-like units. The goal of word tokenization is to convert continuous text into a sequence of discrete tokens, where each token represents a single word or a subword unit.


Whitespace Tokenization:

Input:

"Word tokenization is important!"

output:

["Word", "tokenization", "is", "important!"]

Punctuation Tokenization:

Input:

"Word tokenization is important!"

output:

["Word", "tokenization", "is", "important","!"]


3

Stemming

Stemming is a text processing technique used in Natural Language Processing (NLP) to reduce words to their base or root form, called the "stem." The purpose of stemming is to normalize words that have the same root but might appear in different forms due to inflections, tenses, or derivations. By reducing words to their common base, stemming aims to simplify text analysis and improve the efficiency of language processing tasks.


For example, the stem of the words "running," "runs," and "runner" is "run." Similarly, the stem of "jumps," "jumped," and "jumping" is "jump."

4

Stop word analysis

Stop word analysis, also known as stop word removal, is a common text preprocessing technique used in Natural Language Processing (NLP) to filter out stop words from a given text. Stop words are commonly occurring words in a language that do not carry significant meaning and are often removed to improve the efficiency and focus of language processing tasks.


Examples of stop words in English include "a," "an," "the," "is," "and," "in," "of," "on," etc. These words are frequently used for grammatical purposes but may not contribute much to the overall meaning of a sentence or document.

5

Part-of-speech (POS)

Part-of-speech (POS) refers to the grammatical category or syntactic role that a word plays in a sentence. It is a fundamental concept in linguistics and Natural Language Processing (NLP) that helps in understanding the structure and meaning of a sentence. POS tagging, also known as grammatical tagging or word category disambiguation, is the process of assigning a specific part-of-speech label to each word in a given text.
